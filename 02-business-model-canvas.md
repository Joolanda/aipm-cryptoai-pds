# Activity 2: Business Model Canvas (Draft Level) (90 minutes)

## 🎯 Objective
Create a draft business model for your AI product covering key commercial and operational aspects - enough to guide discovery work, not final detailed planning.

## 📊 Business Model Canvas Overview

The Business Model Canvas has 9 building blocks that describe how your business creates, delivers, and captures value:

```
┌─────────────────┬─────────────────┬─────────────────┬─────────────────┐
│   Key Partners  │  Key Activities │ Value Propositions│Customer Relations│
│                 │                 │                 │                 │
├─────────────────┼─────────────────┤                 ├─────────────────┤
│  Key Resources  │                 │                 │    Channels     │
│                 │                 │                 │                 │
├─────────────────┴─────────────────┼─────────────────┼─────────────────┤
│           Cost Structure          │ Customer Segments│  Revenue Streams │
└───────────────────────────────────┴─────────────────┴─────────────────┘
```

**Approach**: Create draft-level entries for each block - specific enough to be useful, flexible enough to evolve.

## 🔄 Process (90 minutes total)

### Step 1: Customer Segments & Value Propositions (30 minutes)

**Start with WHO and WHY - these drive everything else.**

#### Customer Segments (15 minutes)
Define your target customers (draft level):
- **Primary Segment**: Who needs this most urgently?
- **Key Characteristics**: Role, demographics, context
- **Estimated Size**: Rough market size (thousands? millions?)
- **How to Reach**: Where can you find these users?

**Draft Customer Segment Template:**
```
Primary Segment: [Specific user type]
Characteristics: [Key traits that define them]
Estimated Size: [Rough market size]
Access: [Where to find them]
```

#### Value Propositions (15 minutes)
Define what value you create (draft level):
- **Core Value**: What's the main benefit?
- **Key Problems Solved**: What pain points do you address?
- **AI Advantage**: Why is AI essential for this value?
- **Differentiation**: How is this better than alternatives?

**Draft Value Proposition Template:**
```
Core Value: [Main benefit in one sentence]
Problems Solved: [Top 2-3 pain points addressed]
AI Advantage: [Why AI is necessary]
vs Alternatives: [How you're different/better]
```

### Step 2: Revenue & Customer Relationship (20 minutes)

#### Revenue Streams (10 minutes)
How will you make money (draft approach):
- **Primary Revenue Model**: Subscription? One-time? Usage-based?
- **Rough Pricing**: What might customers pay?
- **Revenue Timeline**: When do payments happen?

**Common AI Product Revenue Models:**
- SaaS subscription (monthly/annual)
- Pay-per-use/API calls
- Freemium with premium features
- One-time purchase
- Data licensing

#### Customer Relationships (10 minutes)
How you connect with customers (draft approach):
- **Acquisition**: How do you get first customers?
- **Retention**: How do you keep them engaged?
- **Support**: What level of service do they expect?

### Step 3: Operations Blocks (25 minutes)

#### Channels (5 minutes)
How customers find and access your product:
- **Discovery**: How do they learn about you?
- **Access**: How do they get/use the product?

#### Key Activities (5 minutes)
Most important things you must do:
- **Product Development**: Building and improving the AI
- **Data Management**: Collecting, processing, updating data
- **Customer Activities**: Marketing, sales, support

#### Key Resources (10 minutes)
Most important assets required:
- **Data**: What data do you need? Where from?
- **Technology**: AI/ML tools, cloud, development resources
- **Human**: What skills/roles are essential?
- **Other**: Any specialized assets needed?

#### Key Partners (5 minutes)
Who you might work with:
- **Data Partners**: Where you get data
- **Technology Partners**: Cloud, APIs, tools
- **Distribution Partners**: How you reach customers

### Step 4: Cost Structure (15 minutes)

#### Major Cost Categories
Most important costs in your business:
- **Development Costs**: Building the product
- **Operational Costs**: Running the service (cloud, data, etc.)
- **Customer Costs**: Marketing, sales, support
- **Rough Monthly Estimate**: What might this cost to run?
